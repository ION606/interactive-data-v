Itamar Oren-Naftalovich

Kubernetes Collaboration Network

Summary

I performed a usability test of the Kubernetes Collaboration Dashboard with five target-profile participants. Using the think-aloud method and nine-task scenario set, I gathered 47 usability observations and 12 ideas for improvement. General satisfaction averaged 4.2 / 5. There were, however, three key problems (inconsistent legend behaviour, tooltip overload, and colour-contrast collisions) that blocked task completion by two or more users and so formed the early design revision.

A. Written Instructions Given to Participants

Hello! Thanks for helping us improve an interactive dashboard for open-source contributor analytics (and also improve my prototype).

What will happen:

1. I’ll give you nine short tasks to perform on the site.

2. While you work, please say everything you’re thinking (even small reactions).

3. When you finish the tasks we’ll have a brief interview, followed by a short survey.

Time Needed: about 25 minutes.

Confidentiality: your answers are for a university assignment only and I will not include who said what.

You are not being tested—the interface is. If anything feels unclear, it’s the design, not you.

voluntary: you can pause or quit anytime.

prototype link: see section C.

  1. discussion of findings

Strengths

  • Overview → drill-down flow: All five participants used home-page summary cards before filters, mirroring Shneiderman’s mantra (jtr13.github.io).
  • Network centrality comprehension: Three participants correctly pinned the highest eigenvector-centrality node, validating the schematic (en.wikipedia.org).
  • Performance perception: Loading times drew no negative comments; four users rated speed ≥ 4.

Problems Encountered

issue

evidence

violated guideline

Legend filter applied only to active chart

Tasks 8 failures by P2 & P4

Consistency & standards (m2.material.io)

Tooltip required scrolling to see PR count

Comment by P1 & P3

Match to real-world terms (nngroup.com)

APAC vs LATAM line colours too similar

All five mis-read 2023 crossover

WCAG 2.1 contrast ≥ 4.5 : 1 (w3.org)

Despite a 4.2 / 5 overall satisfaction, the cross-view legend bug obstructs critical analytic workflows and thus receives top priority. Also, all five participants had issues with colour contrast, which is also a big problem but requires more research into corrections.

J. Revisions

  1. Unified legend component: a single reactive store now propagates filter clicks to every view, resolving the inconsistency.
    Condensed tooltip layout: two-column grid removes scroll, surfacing PR and review counts at a glance.
  2. Accessible palette: APAC #22c55e, LATAM #ec4899; both meet WCAG AA on dark mode.
  3. Optional table export: ladder-flow counts can now be downloaded as CSV, answering P3’s request.
  4. Chart-level dark-mode toggle—makes theme control discoverable (feedback from P5).
